Frequently Asked Questions

Answers about EnteleSCAN, ENK, and the ENTELΞKRON observatory layer.

What is EnteleSCAN?

EnteleSCAN is the intelligence and observability layer of the ENTELΞKRON ecosystem. It provides network exploration, validator intelligence, SOVRA-powered diagnostics, and ecosystem module briefings — more than a standard block explorer.

What is ENK?

ENK (EnteleKRON) is the ERC-20 coordination token of the ENTELΞKRON ecosystem on Ethereum. Max supply is 100 billion with 18 decimals. Contract: 0xC95343B3f8A5af57a9b3B1acFf3D2a7654Fa28F6. See Token Transparency for full details.

Why does on-chain supply show 100B but transfers are locked?

The ENK contract mints the full max supply to a genesis vault. On-chain transfers are restricted until EnteleKRON mainnet launch — the contract reverts with a lock message. EnteleSCAN displays on-chain data for transparency; circulating supply differs from total minted supply.

What do the Mission Control status labels mean?

Qualitative labels (In Progress, Forming, Active) reflect TVK Labs research program status — not performance metrics. Only Explorer Health and API Status reflect live connectivity to Etherscan.

Is EnteleSCAN live on ENTELΞKRON mainnet?

Not yet. v7 indexes Ethereum ENK data. Native ENTELΞKRON chain indexing is planned for v9. The platform is in research and prototype runtime preparation.

How do I become a validator?

The validator program is forming. See the Validator Hub for requirements and contact team@entelescan.com to express interest.

What is SOVRA?

SOVRA (Sovereign Operational Virtual Reasoning Architecture) is the AI intelligence core of the ecosystem. It powers anomaly detection, readiness scoring, and the module briefings on EnteleSCAN. Learn more at sovra.network.

Can I use the API?

Yes. EnteleSCAN exposes REST endpoints for ENK supply, transfers, network stats, and Ethereum search. See the API Reference. Rate limits apply via Etherscan.
